Installation Guide

15 minutes.
One USB drive.

If you can install a printer, you can do this.

What you need

Three things. You probably have them already.

A computer

The one you want to switch. Windows, any age.

A USB drive

8GB or larger. Will be erased.

15 minutes

Mostly waiting. The hard part is three clicks.

1

Download the file

Click the button. Save it anywhere. It's about 3GB.

Download BoringOS

Look for the .iso file in the latest release.

2

Copy it to a USB drive

You need a free tool to do this. We recommend Balena Etcher — it works on Windows, Mac, and Linux.

Using Balena Etcher:

  1. Open Balena Etcher
  2. Click "Flash from file" → select the BoringOS .iso you downloaded
  3. Click "Select target" → choose your USB drive
  4. Click "Flash!" and wait (2-5 minutes)
Balena Etcher screenshot coming soon
3

Restart with USB plugged in

Plug the USB into the computer you want to switch. Restart it.

Most computers will boot from USB automatically. If not, you may need to press a key during startup (usually F12, F2, or Delete) to select the USB drive.

Common boot keys by brand:

  • Dell: F12
  • HP: F9 or Esc
  • Lenovo: F12
  • ASUS: F8 or Esc
  • Acer: F12
4

Answer 5 questions

The installer asks you five things. That's it.

1

Language

What language should menus and apps use?

2

Keyboard

What keyboard layout do you have?

3

Time zone

Where are you located?

4

Your name and password

Create your user account.

5

Which drive

Where to install. Usually there's only one choice.

Done

Remove the USB. Restart. Your computer now runs BoringOS.

Everything is ready. Firefox, email, office apps, photos, music — all installed.

After you install

A few things you might want to do first.

Connect to WiFi

Click the system menu in the top-right corner.

Copy your files

Plug in a USB drive with your documents, photos, music.

Set up email

Open Thunderbird. Add your Gmail, Yahoo, or Outlook account.

Browse the App Store

Thousands of free apps. Click "Software" in the dock.

Questions?

"What happens to Windows?"

It gets replaced. This is a clean install, not a dual-boot. Back up anything you want to keep.

"Can I undo this?"

Yes. You can reinstall Windows later if you want. But most people don't.

"What if something goes wrong?"

The installer is pretty foolproof. If you get stuck, the worst case is you reinstall Windows.

"Do I need to be tech-savvy?"

No. If you can follow these 5 steps, you can do it.
